    Education and Career

    I completed a B.S. (2011) and M.S. (2013) in Mathematical Sciences at KAIST, and a Ph.D. (Aug 2017) in Electrical Engineering at KAIST. From May 2025 to February 28, 2026, I worked on game development at Sayberry Games. Since March 1, 2026, I serve as Assistant Professor at Daejeon University. I am also developing a solo game project, Hexagon Soup (Steam).

    Current Focus

    Core tracks are AI x Games Systems, trustworthy AI, and optimization-based decision making. Papers and implementation outputs are managed together to keep results reproducible and transferable.

    Solo Development In Progress

    Hexagon Soup

    A solo project that connects AI-oriented system design and experimentation to actual player experience. Planning, systems implementation, balancing, and iterative playtesting are all handled end-to-end.

    Research

    Research Areas

    LLM Systems

    LLM-Centric System Architecture

    Designing end-to-end pipelines from requirement definition to deployment with RAG, tool use, and agent orchestration.

    Trustworthy AI

    Reliability and Safety

    Quantifying hallucination, uncertainty, and policy-risk with test benches and setting operational baselines.

    Optimization

    Game Theory and Optimization

    Translating pricing, allocation, and scheduling into mathematical models and automating decisions algorithmically.

    Edge/IoT

    Edge Intelligence

    Deriving practical policies by quantifying latency-cost-quality tradeoffs under resource constraints.

    AI Engineering

    AI-Driven Development

    Converting research code into operational code with experiment automation and release standards.

    Applications

    Applied AI Systems

    Validating outcomes with user-facing metrics in game systems, interactive software, and education tools.
