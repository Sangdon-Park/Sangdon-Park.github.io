About Me

I am a Post Doctoral Researcher at KAIST Information and Electronics Research Institute, specializing in artificial intelligence and computational frameworks. My research focuses on the intersection of AI, natural language processing, and interactive systems, developing innovative solutions that enhance human-AI interaction.

With a strong foundation in both theoretical and applied AI, I explore cutting-edge approaches to create intelligent systems that can understand context, adapt to user behaviors, and generate meaningful responses. My interdisciplinary background in electrical engineering and mathematical sciences provides me with unique perspectives when addressing complex AI challenges.

I am particularly interested in how AI technologies can revolutionize various domains, from energy management systems to interactive digital experiences. My work aims to bridge the gap between theoretical AI research and practical applications that make a positive impact on society.

AI Research

Large Language Models

Exploring the capabilities of large language models to understand context, generate coherent responses, and adapt to specific domains through fine-tuning and prompt engineering techniques.

Conversational AI

Developing modular dialogue management systems with advanced context-tracking mechanisms and sophisticated natural language understanding components for more natural human-AI interactions.

Personalized AI Systems

Designing adaptive AI systems that learn from user interactions, recognize patterns in behavior, and customize responses based on individual preferences and interaction history.

Multimodal AI Integration

Investigating the integration of text, voice, and visual data processing in AI systems to create more comprehensive understanding and responsive interaction models.
