\section{PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY}
Dr. Sangdon Park is an AI and systems researcher whose work connects optimization theory, distributed systems, and practical LLM engineering.
His core interests are edge/network resource optimization, dynamic pricing, and reliable AI deployment in real services.
The current focus is \textbf{AI x Games Systems}, where model quality, latency, cost, and operational stability are optimized together.

He received his Ph.D. from KAIST (2017.08) and then worked as a post-doctoral researcher at KAIST IIER (2017.09--2025.04), conducting research on edge computing, IoT systems, smart-grid optimization, and market-based resource allocation.
During this period, he published journal and conference papers spanning IEEE IoT Journal, IEEE Access, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, and related venues, with continued emphasis on mathematically grounded system design.

From 2025.05 to 2026.02, he worked at Sayberry Games and expanded his research into interactive AI and production-oriented LLM applications.
Since 2026.03.01, he has served as Assistant Professor at Daejeon University, where he teaches Database Systems, Artificial Intelligence, and Capstone Design while continuing research on RAG, tool-use, agent evaluation, and deployable AI systems.
